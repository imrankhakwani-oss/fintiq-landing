What is Monte Carlo simulation and why does it matter for investors?
Monte Carlo simulation is a computational technique that runs thousands of randomly generated market scenarios — based on historical volatility and return data — to show you the full probability distribution of possible outcomes for a stock or portfolio. Rather than a single price forecast that is almost always wrong, you see a range: the 10th percentile (bad scenario), the 50th percentile (median outcome), and the 90th percentile (optimistic scenario). This is how professional risk managers at banks and hedge funds evaluate investments. Fintiq makes this technique available to every retail investor in seconds.
What is Portfolio Optimisation and how does Fintiq use Modern Portfolio Theory?
Portfolio optimisation is the process of selecting the best possible combination of assets to achieve the highest expected return for a given level of risk. Fintiq uses Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT), the framework developed by Nobel Prize-winning economist Harry Markowitz in 1952. By analysing the historical returns, volatility, and correlation between your chosen stocks, Fintiq calculates the mathematically optimal allocation — the point on the Efficient Frontier where return is maximised and risk is minimised. Instead of guessing how much of your portfolio to put in each stock, let the mathematics decide.
What is Pairs Trading and how does Fintiq identify trading pairs?
Pairs trading is a market-neutral quantitative strategy where you simultaneously buy one stock and short-sell a correlated stock when they diverge from their historical relationship, profiting when they converge again. Fintiq uses statistical cointegration analysis — the same methodology used by quantitative hedge funds — to identify pairs of stocks that move together over time. When the price spread between a pair exceeds a statistically significant threshold (measured in standard deviations), Fintiq signals a potential mean-reversion trade. Because you're long one stock and short another, pairs trading can generate returns regardless of whether the broader market is rising or falling.
