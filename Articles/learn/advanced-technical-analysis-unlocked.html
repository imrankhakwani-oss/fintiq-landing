Advanced Technical Analysis: Combining RSI, MACD and Momentum

Updated July 2026 · 20 min read · Advanced

Individual technical indicators are noisy. RSI gives false signals in trending markets. MACD lags. Moving average crossovers whipsaw in range-bound conditions. Professional technical analysts solve this by requiring confluence — multiple independent signals pointing in the same direction simultaneously — before acting. This guide gives you the exact confluence rules used by professional traders.

The Confluence Framework

A high-probability technical setup requires at least 3 of these 5 signals to align:

SignalBullish ConditionBearish ConditionReliability
RSI (14)Rising from <40, crossing 50Falling from >60, crossing 50Medium
MACDBullish crossover below zero lineBearish crossover above zero lineMedium
50/200 EMAPrice above both, 50 above 200Price below both, 50 below 200High (trend)
VolumeRising on up days, falling on down daysRising on down days, falling on up daysHigh (confirmation)
Support/ResistanceBounce from key support levelRejection from key resistance levelHigh (structure)

Multi-Timeframe Analysis — The Professional Approach

Retail investors typically look at one timeframe. Professionals always use at least three:

  • High timeframe (weekly/monthly): Determines the primary trend. Never trade against the weekly trend.
  • Medium timeframe (daily): Identifies entry setups within the primary trend.
  • Low timeframe (4-hour or hourly): Times the exact entry to minimise risk (tighter stop-loss).

The rule: trade in the direction of the high timeframe, enter on the medium timeframe, time with the low timeframe.

Worked Example: BAE Systems (BA.) — 3-Timeframe Setup

  1. Weekly: BA. in an uptrend (50 EMA above 200 EMA, RSI holding above 50 for 6 months). Primary trend: bullish. Only take long setups.
  2. Daily: After a 3-week consolidation, MACD gives a bullish crossover below the zero line. RSI rises from 38 to 52. Volume picks up on the breakout day. Three confluence signals confirmed.
  3. 4-Hour: Price pulls back to the 50 EMA on the 4-hour chart after the daily breakout. RSI touches 48 (minor oversold on short-term). This is the low-risk entry point — stop-loss just below the 4-hour 50 EMA.

Sector Momentum — The Forgotten Edge

Individual stock technical analysis works better when the entire sector is in momentum. A stock in a bullish uptrend within a sector that is also showing strong momentum has a materially higher probability of continuation than a stock in a sector showing weakness. In Fintiq:

  • Check the FTSE 100 sector heatmap for sector momentum
  • Only take long setups in stocks whose sector RSI is above 55
  • Only take short setups in stocks whose sector RSI is below 45
  • Avoid individual stock technical setups that go against sector momentum

Exact Entry, Stop-Loss, and Target Rules

Entry: After 3+ confluence signals confirmed on the daily chart Use 4-hour chart to find a low-risk entry point (pullback to EMA or support) Stop-Loss: Below the most recent swing low (long) or above swing high (short) Never more than 5% from entry on a single stock position Fintiq ATR stop: 2× the 14-day Average True Range below entry Target: Minimum reward-to-risk ratio of 2:1 Primary target: next major resistance level (long) or support (short) Secondary target: measured move (height of the base pattern added to breakout) Position size: Risk no more than 1% of total portfolio on a single technical trade Position size = (1% of portfolio) ÷ (entry price - stop-loss price)

High-Probability Pattern Recognition

The Bull Flag (highest win rate in trending markets)

Anatomy: Strong impulse move up (flagpole) → 1–3 week consolidation in a tight, slightly descending channel (flag) → breakout above the flag on expanding volume. Entry: breakout candle close. Stop: below the flag low. Target: flagpole height added to breakout point.

The Ascending Triangle (best for breakout trades)

Anatomy: Flat resistance being tested multiple times + rising support (higher lows). Interpretation: buyers becoming more aggressive, sellers holding a line. When volume picks up on the breakout above the flat resistance, it's a powerful signal. Entry: breakout candle. Stop: below last higher low. Target: height of triangle added to breakout.

The One Rule That Separates Profitable Technical Traders
Never enter a trade without a defined stop-loss and a pre-calculated position size based on that stop. The mathematics of risk management — never losing more than 1% of capital per trade — means you can be wrong 60% of the time and still be profitable at 2:1 reward-to-risk. The entry setup matters far less than the risk management framework.
