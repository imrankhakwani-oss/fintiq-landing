Monte Carlo Simulation for FIRE and Early Retirement Planning

Updated July 2026 · 20 min read · Advanced

FIRE — Financial Independence, Retire Early — is built on one core question: do I have enough? Monte Carlo simulation is the only rigorous way to answer it, because it replaces a single guess about future returns with a probability distribution across thousands of possible futures.

This guide gives you the complete UK-specific FIRE framework: safe withdrawal rates by age, the State Pension bridge strategy, how to handle inflation, and a step-by-step Fintiq walkthrough for your personal numbers.

The UK State Pension Bridge Strategy

This is the most powerful tool available to UK FIRE retirees and is systematically underused. The logic:

  • State Pension full entitlement: £11,502/year (2025/26), paid from age 66
  • For a couple: £23,004/year — a substantial guaranteed income floor
  • This pension is inflation-linked via the Triple Lock (highest of CPI, earnings growth, or 2.5%)

If you retire at 50 and need £35,000/year, you do NOT need a portfolio that can sustain £35,000 forever. You need:

  • Phase 1 (age 50–66): £35,000/year from portfolio — 16 years
  • Phase 2 (age 66+): £35,000 minus £11,502 State Pension = £23,498/year from portfolio

Running this two-phase scenario in Fintiq's Monte Carlo tool significantly reduces the required portfolio compared to assuming a constant £35,000 withdrawal forever. In practice, the State Pension bridge typically reduces your required FIRE number by 15–25%.

Example: FIRE Number with State Pension Bridge Retire at 50, need £35,000/year, couple with 2x State Pension Without bridge: £35,000 ÷ 3.5% = £1,000,000 required With bridge (Phase 2 withdrawal = £35,000 - £23,004 = £11,996): Phase 1 NPV: £35,000 × 16 years discounted = ~£420,000 Phase 2 NPV: £11,996 / 3.5% = £342,743 (at age 66) Discounted to age 50 at 3.5%: ~£210,000 Total required: approximately £630,000 — 37% less

Sequence of Returns — Protecting Your First 5 Years

The biggest risk to a FIRE plan is a severe market crash in the first 5 years of retirement — before the portfolio has had time to compound through the recovery. Mitigation strategies:

  • Cash buffer: Hold 1–2 years of spending in cash at all times. Draw from this during market downturns rather than selling equities at depressed prices.
  • Flexible withdrawal: If Monte Carlo shows a bad early sequence developing, reduce withdrawals by 10–15% for 1–2 years. This has an outsized positive impact on long-term success rates.
  • Bucket strategy: Bucket 1 (0–3 years spending in cash/bonds), Bucket 2 (3–10 years in balanced funds), Bucket 3 (10+ years in global equities). Refill from Bucket 3 → 2 → 1 during market strength.
  • ISA/SIPP coordination: Draw from ISA first (tax-free) to allow SIPP to compound. Switch at State Pension age when marginal tax rate drops.

Run your Fintiq Monte Carlo with a 35-year horizon, 3.5% withdrawal rate, and a Phase 2 reduction equal to your projected State Pension. If your success probability is above 88%, your plan is robust. If below 80%, you need either more capital, a later retirement date, or a lower spending target.
